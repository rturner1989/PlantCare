Monstera

Monstera deliciosa · Swiss cheese plant

A fast-growing tropical vine from the Central American rainforests. Famous for its fenestrated leaves — the signature holes develop as the plant matures and only if it's getting enough light. Often mistaken for a houseplant that's easy to kill; it's actually forgiving, it just complains loudly.

Care tipswhat actually matters

  • Water when the top 5cm of soil is dry — don't water on a schedule, water on a check. Stick your finger in.
  • Give it a moss pole early. Aerial roots want something to climb. The leaves get bigger and holier when they can climb.
  • Fenestration is earned through light. Bright indirect is the sweet spot. Direct sun scorches; low light means leaves stay round and hole-less.
  • Repot every 18–24 months. Roots circling the pot = it's time. Go one pot size up, not three.

Common issues

Yellow leaves, base of the plant. Old growth dying off is normal — monstera sheds lower leaves as it climbs. If it's widespread, check for overwatering.

Brown crispy leaf edges. Humidity too low, or water too salty. Use filtered water; group with other plants to raise local humidity.

No holes on new leaves. Not enough light, or plant too young. Leaves start fenestrating around leaf 4–6 on most plants.

Related

Part of the Monstera genus — siblings include Monstera adansonii (smaller leaves, same vibe), Monstera siltepecana (silvery juvenile leaves), Monstera dubia (shingling climber). For a non-climbing alternative with similar dramatic cuts: try Philodendron selloum.

Encyclopedia locked

Encyclopedia is where Discover died and Care Tips + Plant Doctor articles grew back in its place. One region for reference material — species + articles — with a clean path from "browse" to "add to my greenhouse". Articles at the top, species grid below. Species cards always show user-ownership status ("You have 1") so the library doubles as a collection overview.

  • Two views. Library (default — search + articles + grid) and Species detail (hero + tips + rail). Deep links to /encyclopedia/:slug and /encyclopedia/articles/:slug.
  • Featured article carousel. Hero card dark forest gradient with sunshine glow (Plant Doctor lead piece) + two lighter companion cards. Articles filed under Plant Doctor (troubleshooting) or Care Tips (general how-to) or Seasonal (time-of-year specific).
  • Filter toolbar. Same primitive as Journal — ⊞ Filters button (mint-tinted + count pill when active), active filters as dismissible badges beside it, Clear all link. Popover on desktop / bottom sheet on mobile. Grouped sections: Category · Light · Water frequency · Safety (Pet-safe/Edible/Air-purifying) · Experience. Sort toggle ("Popular/A-Z/Recently added") on the right of the toolbar. One filter vocabulary across the whole app.
  • Species card. Photo + common name + scientific name + 3 trait chips (water interval · light · pet-safety). "You have N" emerald pill when owned. Always-clickable to detail view.
  • Species detail hero. Large photo. Below: common + scientific name, summary paragraph. No personality badge, no voice sample — personality is never surfaced as a UI label anywhere.
  • Add-to-greenhouse CTA. Dashed-green outlined card at the top of the right rail. Primary action on this whole view. Includes community stats as social proof.
  • Care at a glance. Read-only version of Plant Detail's Schedule panel — Water · Light · Temp · Humidity · Pet safety. Standardised format across every species page so scanning is muscle-memory.
  • Community stat card. Count + rescue-rate + user's own status ("You have 1 · Monty"). Light, not dominant — the Encyclopedia isn't a social network, just a reference.
  • Mobile single-column. Hero → Add CTA → Care facts → tips → community. Back button in topbar replaces breadcrumb. Dock's 5th slot labelled "Library" instead of "Me" when viewing Encyclopedia — shows active context.
