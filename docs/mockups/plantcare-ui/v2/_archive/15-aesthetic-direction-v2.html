What's different from before

Nothing here is "added sparkle" — the whole foundation shifted. Warmer paper-ivory surfaces. Ochre-tinted shadows. Asymmetric portrait panel with real layered depth. Editorial drop caps and small-caps. Soft gradient blobs for depth (no stamps, no cartoons). Two signature gestures (swipe + wheel) as first-class interaction patterns. One dashed flourish per screen, never more.

  • Paper, not white. All card surfaces use #fbf8f1 (warm ivory) and #f3eee2 (deeper paper). Pure white becomes the accent, not the default.
  • Shadows tinted warm. Ochre browns + soft browns, not forest greens. Reads cozier.
  • Depth via blur, not decoration. Three overlapping gradient blobs (mint, sun, coral) behind the viewport. Heavy blur makes them atmospheric.
  • Portraits are layered, not flat. Monstera uses 5 SVG leaves with linear-gradient fills + drop shadows. Approximates 3D-render without needing commissioned assets.
  • Typography does more work. Fraunces handles display and italic body-voice moments. Drop cap anchors the editorial feel.
  • Collection is the character, not the hero. Today shows 5 plants side by side. Each with mood + personality visible. Coral ring on the wilting one.
  • Interaction vocabulary visible. Swipe-to-complete + radial wheel both shown on Today. These are the signature patterns.
  • Coral used deliberately. CTA secondary, "needs water" flourish, overdue tag. Planta uses orange the same way.
